MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE
AGREEMENT
Effective Date: 12 February 2024
This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of the
Effective Date by and between:
Aurelios Systems Inc., a corporation organized under the laws of Delaware with its
principal office at 427 Whitlock Plaza, Wilmington, DE 19803 (“Party A”),
and
Maridian Analytics LLP, a limited liability partnership organized under the laws of New
York with its principal office at 55 Harborview Circle, Brooklyn, NY 11231 (“Party B”).
Each of Party A and Party B may be referred to individually as a “Party” and collectively
as the “Parties.”
1. Purpose
The Parties intend to engage in discussions and exchange information relating to
potential commercial cooperation, technology integration, market evaluation, or other
mutually beneficial initiatives (the “Purpose”).
The exchange of confidential information is necessary for the Parties to evaluate or
further the Purpose.
2. Mutual Confidentiality Obligations
This Agreement imposes mutual confidentiality obligations. Each Party may disclose
Confidential Information to the other, and each Party agrees to protect such information
in accordance with this Agreement.

3. Definition of Confidential Information
“Confidential Information” means any non-public, proprietary, technical, business,
financial, operational, strategic, legal, or commercial information disclosed by a Party
(“Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (“Receiving Party”), whether orally, visually, in
writing, or in electronic form, that:
a. is marked or identified as confidential; or
b. should reasonably be understood to be confidential based on the nature of the
information or the circumstances of disclosure.
Confidential Information includes, without limitation:
● product roadmaps, source code, APIs, system designs
● customer lists, pricing, business plans
● research data, algorithms, workflows
● contracts, financial statements, and projections
● third-party confidential information in the Disclosing Party’s possession
4. Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can
demonstrate:
a. is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;
b. was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to receipt from the Disclosing Party;
c. is independently developed without use of, or reference to, the Confidential
Information; or
d. is obtained lawfully from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation.

5. Obligations of the Receiving Party
The Receiving Party shall:
a. use Confidential Information solely to evaluate or further the Purpose;
b. protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses for
its own confidential information (and no less than reasonable care);
c. restrict disclosure solely to those employees, contractors, and professional advisers
who have a strict need to know for the Purpose;
d. ensure such recipients are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective
than those in this Agreement; and
e. not copy, reverse engineer, disassemble, or create derivative works from Confidential
Information except as permitted by the Purpose.
6. Permitted Disclosures
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:
a. to its employees, directors, contractors, accountants, auditors, financial advisors, or
legal counsel, provided they are bound by obligations of confidentiality at least as
stringent as this Agreement;
b. when required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party
gives prompt notice to the Disclosing Party (to the extent legally permitted) to allow it to
seek protective measures;
c. to its potential investors, financing partners, or acquirers under confidentiality
obligations at least as protective as this Agreement.
7. Confidentiality Period
Confidentiality obligations survive for five (5) years from the date of each disclosure.

8. Information Disclosed Before and
After the Effective Date
This Agreement applies to Confidential Information disclosed before, on, or after the
Effective Date.
9. Third-Party Information
Confidential or proprietary information of third parties disclosed by the Disclosing Party
shall be treated as Confidential Information under this Agreement.
10. Return or Deletion
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or termination of this Agreement, the
Receiving Party shall, within a reasonable time:
a. return or destroy all Confidential Information;
b. delete all electronic records containing Confidential Information; and
c. certify such destruction in writing upon request.
Receiving Party may retain archival copies solely for regulatory compliance or
dispute-resolution purposes.

11. No Licenses or Intellectual Property
Rights
Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any rights or licenses in or to the
Disclosing Party’s intellectual property, except for the limited right to use the Confidential
Information strictly for the Purpose.
12. No Warranty
All Confidential Information is provided “as is”, without any warranties, express or
implied, including warranties of accuracy, completeness, fitness for a particular purpose,
or non-infringement.
13. Remedies
The Parties acknowledge that unauthorized disclosure or misuse of Confidential
Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be
inadequate. The Disclosing Party may seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or
other equitable remedies in addition to any legal remedies available.
14. No Partnership or Agency
Nothing in this Agreement creates any joint venture, partnership, employment, fiduciary,
or agency relationship between the Parties.

15. Term and Termination
This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated by
either Party upon thirty (30) days’ written notice.
Termination does not relieve either Party of obligations regarding Confidential
Information disclosed prior to termination.
16. No Abnormal Restrictions
This Agreement does not impose non-competition, exclusivity, or non-solicitation
obligations unless expressly agreed in a separate written contract.
17. Assignment
Neither Party may assign or transfer this Agreement without the prior written consent of
the other Party, except to an affiliate or to a successor entity in connection with a
merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets.
18. Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the
State of Delaware, without regard to its conflicts-of-laws principles.
19. Amendments
Any amendment or modification of this Agreement must be in writing and signed by both
Parties.

20. Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding
treatment of Confidential Information and supersedes all prior oral or written
agreements concerning confidentiality.
